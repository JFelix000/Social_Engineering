Types of Social Engineering

Social enginnering comes in many forms, some being more time and labor intensive than others.

Phishing

Perhaps the most common example of social engineering seen today is phising. With phishing scams, the individual trying to recieve information from their victum sends malicious content in the form of emails with links or attachmets. Such attacks typically cause the victum to feel a sense of urgency when or fear with the intent of making them act on a matter quickly. Such scams are accompished by impersonating a trusted figure and asking the victum for sensitive or personal informatioon in order to have the issue resolved.

Tailgating

In tailgating, an individual phisically gains access to an area that they have no authoirzation to by following behind an employee who is authorized to enter the area. The individual typically impersonates another figure, whether made up or real, and creates a fabricated story as to why they are there.
